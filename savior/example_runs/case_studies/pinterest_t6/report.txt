[Vulnerability Overview]
================================================================================
ID              : T6
Name            : The Shadow Binding Attack
Risk Level      : High

Description:
After changing the email associated with an OAuth account, a shadow binding is created.
Both the original OAuth account (A) and the new OAuth account (B) can access the same
website account, allowing an attacker to maintain persistent access even after the
victim changes their email.

================================================================================
[Attack Analysis (Site-Specific)]
================================================================================
Attack Path:
1. Logged in with Google OAuth account A and created a Pinterest account
2. Changed the primary email from A to B through account settings
3. Logged out and logged in with Google OAuth account B - accessed the same Pinterest account
4. Logged out and logged in with Google OAuth account A - still accessed the same Pinterest account
5. Both OAuth accounts access the same website account, confirming shadow binding
Missing Security Check: Email change did not unbind original OAuth, creating shadow binding
Proof of Vulnerability: OAuth A and OAuth B both access the same Pinterest account (same user ID, same profile, same settings) after email migration
Identifiers Collected: Username=testuser_pinterest, Profile URL=/testuser_pinterest/, Account settings accessible from both OAuth logins

================================================================================
[General Remediation]
================================================================================
- Unbind the original OAuth account when email is changed
- Require re-authentication with the new OAuth account after email change
- Notify users about OAuth binding status after email change
- Implement proper OAuth account to email binding validation
- Do not allow multiple OAuth accounts to bind to the same website account
